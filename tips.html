Switch to LED Lighting

LED bulbs use up to 75% less energy than incandescent lighting and can last 25 times longer. Replace your most frequently used bulbs first for maximum savings.

Potential savings: ₱80 per year

Unplug Electronics When Not in Use

Even when turned off, many electronics use standby power. Unplugging them or using power strips could save up to 10% on electricity. Focus on TVs, computers, and gaming consoles.

Potential savings: ₱60 per year

Optimize Air Conditioning Temperature

Set your thermostat to 78°F (26°C) in summer and 68°F (20°C) in winter for optimal energy savings. Each degree adjustment can save about 3-5% on energy costs.

Potential savings: ₱105 per year

Use Natural Light

Open curtains and blinds during the day instead of turning on lights. Position desks and seating near windows to maximize natural daylight and reduce the need for artificial lighting.

Fix Leaking Faucets

A faucet that drips at the rate of one drip per second can waste more than 3,000 gallons per year. Checking and fixing leaks promptly can lead to significant water savings.

Potential savings: ₱70 per year

Install Low-Flow Fixtures

Low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators can reduce water use by 30-50% without affecting performance. Modern fixtures provide strong water pressure while using much less water.

Potential savings: ₱95 per year

Full Loads Only

Only run dishwashers and washing machines when they're full to maximize water and energy efficiency. Running half-loads wastes water, electricity, and detergent.

Potential savings: ₱65 per year

Water-Efficient Gardening

Water plants early morning or evening to reduce evaporation. Use drip irrigation instead of sprinklers and consider collecting rainwater for garden use to maximize water savings.
